Cycle the Continental Divide

This is an annual Event taking place Saturday, June 9, 2018. Cycle the Montana portion of the Great Divide Mountain Bike Route.

Ride the longest off-pavement route in the world.

Surfaces range from pavement, good gravel roads, four-wheel-drive roads, single track, or old railroad beds. You will start in Rooseville in the deep woods and near Glacier National Park. You may see grizzly bear, elk, moose, and/ or mountain lions. The Montana section is known for mountainous riding in tall forest with occasional dips into small towns. The route continues to Polaris, then ends in Lakeview, Montana. The total route is 660 miles.

Cost

$100

This covers any permits that are required, biking jersey and any emergency services that are needed.

Additional Information

All bikers must carry water, food, maps spare tire and bike kit with them. You can take as long as you need to over the weekend. Your permits are valid all summer long.
